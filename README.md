# Mission Statement

ChargeFinder Vancouver was developed for the Esri Canada Centre for Excellence (ECCE) 2026 App Challenge.

Our research question:

> How spatially accessible is it to charge your EV in Metro Vancouver, B.C.?

As electric vehicle (EV) adoption grows rapidly in Canada, understanding **real-world accessibility** to charging infrastructure is essential for sustainable transportation planning.

Rather than simply mapping charger locations, our application measures:

- Network-based accessibility  
- Time-of-day traffic conditions  
- Charger-level differentiation  
- Dynamic accessibility variation across a 24-hour period  

---

# App Features

## EV Charging Station Identification
- Click anywhere on the map or enter an address
- Calculate the fastest route to the nearest EV station
- View detailed charging station information

---

## Time-of-Day Accessibility
Our service area analysis integrates real-world traffic conditions to capture temporal fluctuations in accessibility.

Users can:
- Select a specific time of day
- Observe changes in accessibility throughout a 24-hour period
- Visualize peak and off-peak access differences

---

## Charger-Level Differentiation

| Charger Type | Map Color | Description |
|--------------|-----------|------------|
| Level 2 | Yellow | Standard public charging |
| Level 3 (DC Fast) | Red | Fast charging |
| Level 2 & 3 | Blue | Mixed stations |

(Level 1 chargers excluded — home-use only)

# Why This App Matters

## Improving Understanding of EV Accessibility
Infrastructure supply does not equal accessibility.

Accessibility depends on:
- Travel time
- Traffic congestion
- Network connectivity
- Temporal variation

Our app models real-world conditions.

---

## Supporting Emissions Reduction
Transportation electrification plays a critical role in reducing greenhouse gas emissions. By improving EV charging access, we support sustainable mobility goals.

---

## Long-Term Infrastructure Planning
As EV adoption expands beyond early adopters, equitable infrastructure distribution becomes increasingly important. Our tool helps identify spatial gaps in charging access.
